Tattoo aftercare: how to heal your new tattoo right

A great tattoo can be ruined by poor healing, and a simple piece can stay beautiful for decades with good aftercare. Here's the no-nonsense version of how to look after a fresh tattoo.

The first few hours

Your artist will cover the tattoo before you leave. Keep that initial wrap on for the time they tell you. When you remove it, wash your hands first, then gently clean the tattoo with lukewarm water and a mild, fragrance-free soap. Pat it dry with a clean paper towel. Never rub it.

The first two weeks

Wash the tattoo gently twice a day. Apply a thin layer of the aftercare product your artist recommends. Thin is the key word, the skin needs to breathe. Too much product traps moisture and slows healing.

What to avoid while healing

  • Don't pick or scratch, even when it peels and itches. This is the hardest rule and the most important.
  • Stay out of pools, the sea, and long baths. Showers are fine.
  • Keep it out of direct sun. In Thane's climate this matters, sun on a healing tattoo fades and damages it.
  • Avoid heavy sweating and intense gym sessions for the first several days.
  • Wear loose clothing over the area so nothing rubs against it.

The itchy, peeling stage

Around days four to fourteen the tattoo will flake and peel like a healing sunburn. This is completely normal. Let it happen naturally. Keep moisturising lightly and resist the urge to help it along.

Long term

Once healed, the single best thing you can do for your tattoo is sunscreen. Sun is the main reason tattoos fade over the years. A bit of SPF on exposed ink keeps it sharp for decades.
